Design Principles

Hick's Law

Google

Google.com

Google does an excellent job of keeping their home page simple. The only real option is to input your question.
Their are buttons for you to click that will take your to other google products such as google drive and gmail.
Due to the size and location that they are at they don't pull my attention away from the reason I came to the website.
This makes the website incredibly easy to use no matter the skill level of the user.

Fitt's Law

Youtuble

youtube.com

Youtube is designed to maximize your time watching videos. The best way to do that is making it easy to
click of videos. Each video has a thumbnail and a title, and you can navigate to that video by clicking on either.
The channels you are subscribed to are in a neat list on the side of the page.

White Space and Clean Design

Netflix

Netflix.com

Netflix has tons of shows and movies so desplaying them neatly is very important. As you move down the page they
group shows into different catigories but the catigories are seperated by enough white space that you will never get
confused at which section you are looking at.
